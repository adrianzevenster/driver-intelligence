# Miami Weather Patterns
Miami in May is hot and humid — conditions comparable to Singapore but in an afternoon slot rather than at night. Air temperatures reach 28–34°C, track surface can exceed 50°C. Afternoon thunderstorm risk is significant; Florida convective storms can develop rapidly and are hard to predict precisely.

Conditions:
- Wet risk: Moderate to High. Afternoon convective thunderstorms are common in South Florida in May. The 2022 and 2023 races were dry, but practice and qualifying have seen rain. Engineers should have intermediate tire strategy planned from lap 1.
- Temperature: Hottest race alongside Singapore. Track temp 48–55°C. Tire thermal degradation is elevated; softs cliff earlier than European spring circuits.
- Humidity: 65–80%. Cockpit conditions are demanding; driver fatigue factor higher than at dry desert circuits.
- Wind: Gusty sea breeze, variable 15–30 km/h. Can affect car balance through Turns 15–17 back straight.

Strategy implications: If clouds or rain develop during the race, the track surface cools rapidly. A driver on hard compound with marginal pace may benefit from staying out under changing conditions as tire performance equalises. The rain/dry transition is a high-stakes strategic inflection point unique to Miami in the current calendar.
