# Interlagos ERS Deployment
Interlagos has two primary deployment zones and good harvest from the Senna S braking complex. The short lap length means more ERS cycles per race distance than most circuits.

Key deployment zones:
- Reta Oposta (back straight, Turns 8 to 9): Primary deployment. From Laranjinha exit through the long back straight to Mergulho braking. DRS zone; adds 0.3–0.4 s.
- Main straight (Junção exit to Senna S): Secondary deployment from Junção exit through pit straight to Senna S braking. DRS zone.

Key harvest zones:
- Senna S (Turns 1–2): Heavy braking entry; high regen yield. Primary harvest.
- Mergulho braking (Turn 9): Good regen from back-straight speed.
- Bico de Pato (Turn 11): Medium regen.

Battery management thresholds:
- SOC below 0.22 before Reta Oposta (Turn 7 exit): reduce deployment to 70% on back straight, maintain main-straight deployment.
- SOC below 0.12: full harvest. Alert engineer.
- Altitude note: Interlagos is at 760m altitude. The thinner air reduces combustion power and also reduces brake cooling efficiency. Brake temperatures run slightly higher than sea-level models predict; add 5–8% to expected brake temperature estimates.
