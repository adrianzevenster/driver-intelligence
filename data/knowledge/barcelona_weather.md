# Barcelona Weather Patterns
The Spanish Grand Prix in May sees warm, mostly dry Mediterranean conditions. Track temperatures regularly reach 45–55°C in afternoon qualifying, which is among the highest of the season. Rainfall is rare but possible; wind from the sea occasionally creates gusty conditions in sector 3.

Conditions:
- Dry and hot: Standard. The high track temperature significantly accelerates front-left degradation relative to pre-season testing (which occurs in February at 20–25°C). Tire degradation at the race will be 30–40% higher than testing data at the same circuit. Discount testing compound models when planning race strategy.
- Light rain: Rare; when it occurs it is usually associated with a coastal trough in the evening. Surface dries quickly in the Mediterranean climate.
- Wind: Southeasterly sea breeze in the afternoons can reach 20–25 km/h. This affects the final sector (Turns 12–16) most, where the track is exposed. Cross-wind through the main straight is a minor DRS wobble risk above 20 km/h.

Track evolution: Barcelona is used for testing, so the track surface is more rubberised than most circuits at race weekend. Early evolution is therefore less dramatic — the circuit is already partially rubbered. However, the clean-sweep after qualifying means lap 1 of the race starts with less rubber than expected. Evolution resumes from lap 5–8.
