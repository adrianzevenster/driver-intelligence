# Abu Dhabi Weather Patterns
Yas Marina in November is among the most predictable weather circuits on the calendar. The race is held at sunset with the second half run under floodlights. No rain risk. Track temperature at race start is approximately 33°C and falls to 26–28°C by the final laps.

Conditions:
- Dry: Always. Abu Dhabi has not experienced rain-affected racing in the Yas Marina era.
- Temperature gradient: The most significant weather variable. The 5–7°C temperature drop from race start to finish extends compound windows progressively through the stint. A tire that was marginal at lap 20 in hot conditions may recover performance at lap 35 as temperatures fall. This creates a distinctive tire behaviour pattern unique to Abu Dhabi.
- Humidity: Coastal humidity (50–60%) is moderate. This is not a Singapore-level thermal stress event for drivers; cockpit temperatures stay manageable through the evening race window.

Wind: Occasional light offshore breezes (10–15 km/h). No meaningful wind impact on DRS stability or tire wear at typical Abu Dhabi wind speeds.

Strategy implications of temperature gradient: A driver who is managing a degrading tire at lap 25 in warm conditions may not need to pit as urgently as the wear rate implies. The falling temperature will extend the compound by 4–8 laps compared to a circuit held at stable temperature. Programme this decay reduction into the pit window calculation — do not close the window as early as a constant-temperature analysis suggests.
