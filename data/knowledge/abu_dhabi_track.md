# Abu Dhabi Track Intelligence
Yas Marina is the season-finale circuit and a relatively forgiving track for tires in the cooler November evening conditions. Three DRS zones provide overtaking opportunities at multiple points. The circuit's character changed significantly after the 2021 redesign — slower, more technical marina section was replaced with faster flowing corners, improving racing.

Key sectors:
- Sector 1: Turns 1–9, wide open section through the marina hotel. Turn 1 heavy braking (primary overtaking zone), Turns 5–7 medium-speed sequence. Relatively low tire load; front-left takes the most stress through Turns 5–6.
- Sector 2: Turns 10–14, inner section. Turn 11 hairpin (secondary overtaking point under DRS). The slow Turns 13–14 chicane replaced the old marina tunnel section; provides tactical undercut opportunity.
- Sector 3: Turns 15–21, return to straight. Three linked corners (Turns 17–19) replaced the old slow section; now higher-speed and create moderate rear load. Turn 21 final corner — traction here sets up main straight DRS.

Intervention notes: Abu Dhabi is generally a one-stop race with medium tire degradation. In cooler night conditions (track 25–30°C by race end), compound windows are 20–30% longer than equivalent European summer races. Monitor battery deployment carefully across all three DRS zones — battery depletion risk is higher here than most circuits due to three deployment zones.
