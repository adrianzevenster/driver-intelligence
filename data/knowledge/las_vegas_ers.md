# Las Vegas ERS Deployment
Las Vegas has the longest main straight of any current circuit, delivering the highest single-lap ERS deployment value. The cold night temperatures also affect battery performance — cold batteries deliver current less efficiently, which can reduce peak deployment slightly in the opening laps.

Key deployment zones:
- Main straight / Strip (Turn 17 to Turn 1): Primary DRS zone; full deployment from Turn 17 exit; reaches 340+ km/h. Adds approximately 0.6 s — highest value on the calendar alongside Monza.
- Back straight (Turn 12 to Turn 14): Secondary zone; adds approximately 0.3 s.

Key harvest zones:
- Turn 1 braking: The hardest single braking event in F1; deceleration from 340+ km/h; outstanding energy recovery.
- Turn 12 braking (chicane entry): Hard stop from back straight; good harvest.
- Sector 2 hairpins: Multiple lower-speed braking events; moderate combined harvest.

Battery management thresholds:
- Cold battery in first 3 laps: SOC discharge rate may be lower than nominal due to cold electrolyte. Do not assume nominal deployment is available on lap 1–2; advise driver to reduce deployment demand and allow battery temperature to normalise.
- Nominal SOC entering Strip straight: 0.55+. The enormous straight length requires more SOC than any other circuit.
- SOC below 0.40: reduce back straight deployment; protect Strip straight.
- SOC below 0.25: Strip straight only; full harvest on Turn 1 and chicane braking.
- SOC below 0.12: full harvest mode. Alert engineer.

Warm-up protocol: Battery thermal management should be set to aggressive pre-warm on the formation lap to counteract the cold ambient. Confirm with engineer before race start that battery operating temperature has reached nominal range.
