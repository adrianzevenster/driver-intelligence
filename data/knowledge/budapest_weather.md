# Budapest Weather Patterns
The Hungarian Grand Prix is held in late July, making it one of the hottest and most humid events on the European calendar. The Hungaroring sits in a natural bowl outside Budapest; there is minimal wind and the heat builds throughout the day.

Conditions:
- Dry: Almost always. Hungary rarely sees rain during race day; occasional wet qualifying sessions occur but the race has been dry in most recent years.
- Temperature: Air 28–36°C, track 48–58°C. Among the highest track temperatures on the European calendar alongside Barcelona. Tire thermal degradation is extreme; softs degrade in under 10 laps.
- Humidity: 40–60%. Elevated but not Singapore-level. Cockpit conditions are demanding over the full race distance.
- Haze and low-wind: The bowl topography traps heat. Air quality and temperature build through the race day.

Compound strategy: The extreme track temperature means the soft compound is rarely competitive beyond 8–12 laps. Medium → hard one-stop is the baseline; two-stop medium → medium → hard is common when degrades are high. The heat also amplifies rear tire wear at the slow-speed hairpins — models should use an increased wear coefficient relative to cooler conditions.
