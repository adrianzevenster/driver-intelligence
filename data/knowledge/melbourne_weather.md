# Melbourne Weather Patterns
Melbourne in March (Australian autumn) is famously unpredictable. The city can experience four seasons in one day: warm sunshine in the morning, thunderstorms in the afternoon, and cool evenings. The Australian Grand Prix has seen both fully dry races and severely disrupted wet conditions.

Conditions:
- Dry: Standard. Albert Park's smooth asphalt responds well to slick compounds. Soft compound tires overheat on the abrasive sections (Turns 1–3) faster than at other smooth circuits.
- Rain: Melbourne rain arrives quickly. Local weather systems move faster than forecast models predict at trackside. Intermediates are the first call; the circuit responds to drying quickly due to the smooth surface and good drainage near the lake.
- Cool and overcast: Common in late March. Track temperatures below 20°C mean tire warm-up is slow. The first 3 laps on cold slicks are critical — inform driver to build trust gradually.

Safety car interaction: Melbourne's safety car rate means tire window planning should account for free stops. In wet conditions, safety car deployment is near-guaranteed whenever barriers are hit; plan pit window flexibility accordingly.

Track evolution: Albert Park evolves later in the season than circuits used year-round. Lap 10 is typically when the circuit reaches its optimum. The opening laps on fresh-rubber are slower here relative to season potential than almost any other circuit.
