# Interlagos Track Intelligence
The Autódromo José Carlos Pace is the most elevation-varied circuit on the calendar. The anti-clockwise layout creates unusual tire loading — left-front and right-rear carry more load than at clockwise circuits. The circuit is short (4.3 km) with frequent braking events and a famous Reta Oposta back straight. Safety car frequency is high due to the challenging mixed-speed character and tropical weather.

Key sectors:
- Sector 1: Turns 1–3, descent from Senna S. The Senna S (Turns 1–2) under braking is the highest-frequency near-miss zone at Interlagos. Turn 3 Curva do Sol (long fast left) is the highest-load left-front corner.
- Sector 2: Turns 4–8, Descida do Lago and Laranjinha. These medium-speed corners create rear-right load (anti-clockwise circuit). Rear-right is the primary degradation tire at Interlagos.
- Sector 3: Turns 9–12, Mergulho, Pinheirinho, Bico de Pato, and Junção. The Reta Oposta back straight starts after Junção; traction from Junção defines straight-line performance.

Intervention notes: at Interlagos, rear-right is the primary wear tire (anti-clockwise). If rear-right exceeds 60% before lap 22 on Medium compound, pit window is open. Left-front also loads under Senna S braking. The safety car rate means a two-stop strategy often becomes equivalent to one-stop; keep the second-stop option available until lap 35.
