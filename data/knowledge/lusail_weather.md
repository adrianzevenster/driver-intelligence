# Qatar Weather Patterns
The Qatar Grand Prix at Lusail is run as a night race in October. Qatar at this time of year is hot by European standards, but begins to transition out of the extreme summer heat. The race starts in daylight (or dusk) and concludes at night, creating a notable temperature gradient during the race.

Conditions:
- Dry: Always. Qatar's desert climate means no rain risk at any point of the October racing window.
- Temperature: Air 28–36°C at race start, dropping to 24–30°C by the final laps as night develops. Track temperature falls from ~45°C to ~32°C. The temperature drop through the race is significant and progressive.
- Dust and sand: Desert dust can affect track grip levels in low-wind conditions; grip typically improves through the weekend as the racing line is swept clean.
- Humidity: Very low (10–25%); dry desert air with no moisture-related tire behaviour.

Tire strategy in falling temperatures: The progressive temperature drop mirrors the Abu Dhabi pattern. A tire that appears marginal at lap 20 with a 45°C track surface will have extended life as the track surface cools toward 32°C. Do not trigger a pit stop based on early wear projections without applying the temperature decline forecast. The second stint on medium or hard compound will consistently outperform the first-stint degradation trend, making the overcut more viable at Qatar than at constant-temperature circuits.

Driver health note: Qatar humidity is the lowest of any race, but heat prior to the cool-down is significant. The 2023 Qatar sprint and race weekend saw multiple driver hydration and heat-related incidents; monitor driver core temperature reporting if available.
