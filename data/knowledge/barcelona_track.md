# Barcelona Track Intelligence
The Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya is the most comprehensively tested circuit on the calendar — all teams complete pre-season testing here. The track demands strong front-left performance through constant-radius high-speed corners (Turns 3, 9, 12). Front-left degradation at Barcelona is the defining strategy constraint, mirroring the Silverstone challenge but at slightly lower peak load.

Key sectors:
- Sector 1: Turns 1–4, medium-to-high speed. Turn 3 (Renault, long right-hander) creates sustained front-left load at moderate speed. Understeer here builds front-left heat.
- Sector 2: Turns 5–9, challenging high-speed section. Turn 5 (Seat, long hairpin) is rear-load dominant; Turn 9 (Campsa) is fast right where front-left load peaks. This is the sector where front-left degradation gradient is highest.
- Sector 3: Turns 10–16, varied medium speed. Turn 10 heavy braking; Turns 12–13 chicane. Turn 16 final hairpin — traction out of here sets up the DRS main straight.

Intervention notes: front-left wear at Barcelona is the dominant decision variable. If FL wear exceeds 58% before lap 20 on Medium compound, open pit window. Axle imbalance (FL-RL difference > 14%) is a reliable early warning for corner-entry instability developing. Circuit grip evolves significantly during the race; do not use lap 5 sector times as degradation baseline — use lap 10 onwards.
