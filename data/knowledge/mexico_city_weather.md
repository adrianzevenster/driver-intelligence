# Mexico City Weather Patterns
The Mexican Grand Prix is held in late October in one of the world's largest cities at 2,240 metres altitude. Afternoon thunderstorms are common in Mexico City's October rainy season, and the altitude produces a distinctive weather pattern: clear mornings followed by storm development in the afternoon.

Conditions:
- Wet risk: Moderate to High. October is at the tail end of Mexico's rainy season. Afternoon convective storms have disrupted qualifying and practice sessions. The 2019 race weekend saw intermittent rain.
- Temperature: Air 16–22°C — cool by F1 standards, even though it is October. The altitude means the air temperature is much lower than equatorial circuits at similar latitudes. Track temperature 22–32°C.
- Storm development: Local forecasters track cumulus development over the city; mountain thunderstorms tend to move toward the circuit by 3–4 pm local time.

Tire strategy implications: The cool temperatures make tire warm-up a significant factor, similar to Shanghai or Imola. Medium compound warm-up laps may require 2–3 laps longer to reach operating temperature than at sea-level hot circuits. Degradation rates are also lower than models calibrated at warmer circuits; use a temperature-adjusted wear coefficient. If rain arrives, the cool baseline conditions mean the track dries relatively slowly compared to hot circuits.
