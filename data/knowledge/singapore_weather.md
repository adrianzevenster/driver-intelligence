# Singapore Weather Patterns
Singapore in September is consistently hot (31–33°C) and extremely humid (75–90% relative humidity). Tropical thunderstorms are possible at any point but most likely in the mid-race window (laps 20–40 in a 61-lap race) based on historical data. The night race reduces temperature by only 2–3°C compared to afternoon conditions.

Conditions:
- Dry: Standard. The combination of heat and humidity makes this the most physically demanding dry race on the calendar.
- Light rain: The street circuit becomes immediately treacherous in light rain. Intermediates are required as soon as rain is detected on the circuit. The smooth asphalt and constant drainage sluices mean water clears quickly — the drying phase is fast once rain stops.
- Heavy tropical downpour: Red flag risk is high. If race is red-flagged, tire strategy resets. Plan for this contingency at Singapore more than any other circuit.

Humidity effects on tires: High humidity increases tire surface moisture slightly even in "dry" conditions during humid phases. Effective grip may be 5–8% lower than equivalent lap time model predicts at lower-humidity circuits. Adjust intervention grip threshold down to 0.65 from typical 0.68.

Safety car: Multiple safety cars per race is common. Plan pit windows in two or three potential windows rather than committing to a single plan.
