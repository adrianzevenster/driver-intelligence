# Red Bull Ring Weather Patterns
The Austrian Grand Prix is held in late June or early July in the Styrian Alps. The mountain setting makes the weather among the most unpredictable on the calendar. Afternoon thunderstorms develop rapidly over the mountains and can transform a dry race into a wet one within minutes.

Conditions:
- Wet risk: High. The Red Bull Ring is notorious for sudden storms. The 2021 Styrian GP saw significant rain. Lightning risk is also a safety consideration — red flag risk is higher than any other circuit on the calendar.
- Temperature: Air 18–28°C, track 30–45°C in dry sunny conditions. Mountain altitude means air temperatures drop sharply in cloud cover.
- Variability: Mountain weather is not predictable beyond a 2-hour window. Any radar signal that the team reads in the morning session may be irrelevant by race time.
- Alpine thunderstorms can produce hail as well as heavy rain; circuit wet conditions can be severe.

Strategy implications: The Red Bull Ring safety car probability (from weather and collision) is among the highest on the calendar. Engineers should model pit windows that preserve flexibility for a free pit stop under safety car from lap 10 onwards. A one-stop race that encounters a safety car at lap 20 can become a potential zero-stop race on hard compound if strategically managed correctly.
