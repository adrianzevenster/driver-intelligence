# Suzuka Weather Patterns
Suzuka is held in September or October, a period of transitional weather in Japan. Typhoon-adjacent weather systems can deliver heavy rain with short notice. When dry, track temperatures are moderate (25–40°C), making tire performance predictable.

Conditions and tire crossover:
- Dry: Standard compound selection. Medium and Hard are the preferred race compounds given the balanced four-tire wear profile.
- Light rain: The S-curves become critical in light rain. Intermediates are required as soon as measurable rain falls in sector 1 — do not attempt to extend slick stint through the S-curves in damp conditions. Even 20% wet means the sustained loading through the esses will induce a high-speed excursion.
- Heavy rain: Full Wet mandatory if standing water at Spoon or the back straight. 130R is not safely navigated in heavy rain even on Full Wet; Safety Car is expected and appropriate.

Track evolution: Suzuka rubbers in more slowly than most circuits due to the low-frequency nature of the mixed-speed layout. Expect consistent 0.5–0.8 s improvement through the first 20 laps as rubber is laid down.

Crosswind at 130R: Any crosswind above 20 km/h perpendicular to 130R (from the east, off Ise Bay) significantly reduces lateral stability margin. If wind is forecast, alert driver to reduce commitment at 130R by 3–5% and monitor axle imbalance for rear instability indicator.

Temperature drop: If the race runs into evening (night lighting at Suzuka for some events), track temperature can fall 8–12°C in the final 15 laps. This reduces tire thermal load and can actually improve wear rates, but cold tires on the opening lap after a safety car are a significant grip hazard.
