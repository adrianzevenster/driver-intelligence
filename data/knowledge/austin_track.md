# Austin (COTA) Track Intelligence
The Circuit of the Americas is a purpose-built facility with the most varied character on the calendar. Sector 1 mirrors Suzuka's S-curves with high-speed direction changes. Turn 1 is the principal overtaking zone — the highest point on the circuit with a long braking zone. The circuit is tire-balanced with no single axle dominating degradation, similar to Suzuka.

Key sectors:
- Sector 1: Turn 1 heavy braking (highest point), Turns 2–9 S-curves. The S-curve complex demands four-corner balance — all tires load simultaneously. Confidence in the S-curves on worn tires is the key driver communication metric.
- Sector 2: Turns 10–15, medium-speed esses and hairpin. Turn 12 is the key rear-load corner; rear-right specifically. Hairpin at Turn 11 is the secondary undercut vulnerability.
- Sector 3: Turns 16–20, return to the start-finish. Turn 19 slow hairpin; Turn 20 fast sweeper onto the straight. Traction out of Turn 20 is the final tire-load event and sets up overtaking into Turn 1 next lap.

Intervention notes: COTA's four-corner wear means axle imbalance is less diagnostic than at single-axle circuits. Monitor overall wear rate versus expected compound model. If sector 1 S-curve confidence falls (driver reports instability in Turns 4–8), that is a compound wear signal more than a setup signal. Threshold: grip estimate below 0.67 triggers review.
