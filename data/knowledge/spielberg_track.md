# Red Bull Ring (Spielberg) — Track Characteristics
The Red Bull Ring in Spielberg, Austria is the shortest circuit on the F1 calendar at 4.318 km. Despite its brevity, it is extremely demanding due to significant elevation changes and only two real heavy braking zones, which concentrates the tire and brake stress into a small number of corners. The circuit sits at 660m altitude in the Styrian hills.

Key sectors:
- Sector 1: Start to Turn 3 (Remus); main straight leads to Turn 1 (not a braking zone, it is a fast crest approach); Turn 3 is the primary braking zone, heavy stop from 300 km/h downhill
- Sector 2: Sweeping Turns 4–6; high lateral load on the rear; elevation changes through the back section
- Sector 3: Final hairpin (Turn 9 — Rindt Corner) leads onto the main straight; the only clean overtaking point; heavy braking from 290 km/h

Tire behaviour:
- Two primary braking zones per lap means brake fade risk is concentrated; monitor brake temperature carefully.
- Rear tires take high lateral load through turns 4–6 on the elevation change; rear degradation is the limiting factor.
- Soft compound can run 12–18 laps in Austrian summer temperatures; medium is standard for a clean one-stop race.
- High altitude reduces aerodynamic efficiency, which requires setup adjustments and changes tire load distribution.

Overtaking: Turn 3 (sector 1 braking zone) and Turn 9 (hairpin) are the two overtaking points. Passing is more achievable at Red Bull Ring than at Monaco or Zandvoort. DRS on the main straight creates an effective attack zone into Turn 3.
