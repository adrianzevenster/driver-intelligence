# Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari (Imola) — Track Characteristics
Imola is one of the most historic circuits on the calendar — a narrow, traditional layout with minimal run-off and no real room for error. The track runs anti-clockwise, which reverses tire loading relative to most circuits and puts unusual demands on the right-front. Overtaking is extremely limited; qualifying position and safety car timing are often the decisive strategic factors.

Key sectors:
- Sector 1: Tamburello right-hander complex (fast, high downforce), Villeneuve chicane (heavy braking), Tosa hairpin (key overtaking point)
- Sector 2: Piratella (fast left), Acque Minerali chicane, high-speed section; rear stability critical
- Sector 3: Variante Alta (slow chicane), Rivazza (right-hander, fast exit), main straight DRS

Tire behaviour:
- Anti-clockwise layout means the right-front takes the highest lateral load — the opposite of most circuits. This shifts the wear balance from the traditional left-side to the right axle.
- Softs are qualifying-only; medium and hard required for the race.
- Traction at Tosa and Rivazza loads the rear significantly.
- The narrow circuit and limited overtaking reduce aggressive tire management variety.

Overtaking and strategy notes:
- Tosa (Turn 4) is the only reliable overtaking point into a heavy braking zone.
- Virtual safety cars are common at Imola due to debris on tight straights.
- Pit window is narrow: overcut and undercut advantages are roughly equal given the difficulty of overtaking on track.
- Two-stop strategies are rare unless safety car creates a free pit stop opportunity.
