# Interlagos Weather Patterns
The São Paulo Grand Prix in November faces the start of the Brazilian wet season. Late afternoon thunderstorms are among the most reliable weather events in F1 — the probability of rain during a race window at Interlagos in November exceeds 60% in most years. The 2016 and 2023 races were partially run under safety car in severe conditions.

Conditions:
- Dry: Possible but do not treat it as guaranteed. Strategy must always incorporate a wet-weather contingency plan.
- Rain mid-race: The most common pattern. Rain arrives during laps 20–40 (late afternoon Brazilian time). Intermediate crossover should be called immediately on confirmed rainfall. Do not wait to verify grip — by the time grip falls below threshold at Interlagos, the tire delta is already race-decisive.
- Heavy tropical storm: Safety car or red flag. The circuit can drain but the pit straight is exposed; heavy rain creates standing water quickly. If red flag is expected, manage tire condition to minimise compound usage before the reset.

Altitude effects: At 760 m, adiabatic cooling means cloud formation accelerates. Weather forecasts for Interlagos should use +15 km/h windspeed for cloud movement estimates compared to sea-level circuits. Storms arrive faster than standard forecast models predict.

Track evolution: Interlagos rubber evolves well in dry conditions but is completely reset by rain. A mid-race wet interval followed by drying produces a two-phase race where tire selection at the restart is independent of the pre-rain strategy.
