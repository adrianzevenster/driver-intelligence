# Shanghai International Circuit — Track Characteristics
Shanghai is a technically demanding circuit with three very distinct sectors. The stadium section in sector 1 uses a long entry into the hairpin complex (Turns 1–2) that tests tire management — the slow tight corner and immediate long sustained right-hander causes significant front-left wear. Sector 3 features a long back straight and very long final corner (Turn 16) that loads the rear tires into the braking zone.

Key sectors:
- Sector 1: wide, fast approach into Turns 1–2 snail hairpin; then Turns 3–5 sweeping complex; front-axle dominant
- Sector 2: slow-speed technical section through stadium S-section; Turn 6 heavy braking; mechanical grip prioritised
- Sector 3: back straight DRS zone to Turn 14 braking; long final corner Turn 16 (radius increases); rear loading

Tire behaviour:
- High front-left wear is the defining characteristic. The Turns 1–2 hairpin combined with sector 1 sweepers loads the FL axle significantly.
- Soft compound degrades quickly in sector 1; medium and hard are preferred race compounds.
- Turn 16 long final corner causes rear tire temperature build-up; monitor RR wear independently.
- One-stop and two-stop strategies both viable depending on safety car deployment.

Key corners for engineers:
- Turn 1 entry: Braking reference for locking tendency; monitor brake balance.
- Turn 14 braking: Hard stop from ~310 km/h; brake fade risk on high-downforce cars.
- Turn 16 final sector exit: Rear snap on egress at high fuel load.
