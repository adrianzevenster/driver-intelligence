# Baku ERS Deployment
Baku offers the largest single deployment zone on the calendar. The 2.2 km main straight requires maximum deployment for competitive straight-line speed. The tight old-city sector provides good brake-regen harvest.

Key deployment zones:
- Main straight (Turn 16 to Turn 1): Full deployment from Turn 16 exit through entire 2.2 km straight. This is the most valuable single ERS zone in F1. Cars reach 365+ km/h; power unit and ERS capacity are fully utilised. Adds 0.5–0.8 s per lap.
- Boulevard back straight (Turns 4–5): Secondary deployment; moderate gain.

Key harvest zones:
- Turn 1 braking from 365 km/h: Highest-energy single braking event in F1. Substantial regen; typically yields 20–25% SOC per lap.
- Old-city sector (Turns 7–10): Continuous low-speed braking and acceleration. High harvest efficiency relative to lap time.

Battery management thresholds:
- SOC below 0.30 entering Turn 16: critical alert — main straight deployment must be reduced. This is rare but possible if harvest failed. Loss of full main-straight deployment costs 0.7+ s, which is race-decisive.
- SOC below 0.15: full harvest. Alert engineer urgently.
- Wind note: headwind on main straight above 20 km/h changes the effective deployment profile. Car reaches peak speed later, extending the deployment window slightly. Tailwind reverses this. Adjust mental model accordingly.
