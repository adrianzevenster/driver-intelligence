# Austin (COTA) Weather Patterns
The United States Grand Prix in October experiences highly variable central Texas weather. It can be hot (30°C+), cool (15°C), wet, or dry within the same race weekend. Afternoon thunderstorms are a regular risk in October; the circuit has experienced race stoppages and red flags due to weather.

Conditions:
- Hot and dry: Track temperatures can reach 50°C in afternoon heat. This is the dominant condition in recent years (with night lighting for portions of the race).
- Cool and dry: Cold fronts in October can drop temperatures to 15–18°C overnight, dramatically extending compound windows relative to practice data.
- Rain: Texas thunderstorms arrive with little warning and produce intense but often brief rainfall. Immediate Intermediate call is correct when rain reaches the circuit; the surface drains quickly.
- Overnight temperature drops: For sprint/evening races, temperature can drop 12–15°C from race start to end. Monitor compound window — tires that were degrading may stabilise or recover grip as temperature falls.

Wind: Central Texas wind can be significant (25–30 km/h). No dominant direction; check forecast. Crosswind on the main straight above 20 km/h affects DRS stability. Headwind to Turn 1 from the high point increases braking distance.
