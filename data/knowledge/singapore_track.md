# Singapore Track Intelligence
Marina Bay is a night street circuit in tropical conditions. The heat and humidity are the primary performance variables — drivers and cars lose performance through thermal stress. Tire wear is moderate but braking zones are severe. Safety car frequency is high (comparable to Melbourne and Baku). Undercut is the dominant pit strategy because overtaking on the narrow circuit is nearly impossible.

Key sectors:
- Sector 1: Turns 1–9, Marina Bay waterfront. Turn 1 heavy braking, Turns 3–5 twisting sequence. These corners define the entry instability risk; worn front-right at Singapore causes understeer at Turn 3 entry.
- Sector 2: Turns 10–18, Anderson Bridge and Esplanade. The complex through Turns 14–17 (Esplanade) is the key undercut vulnerability zone — a small gap here can be closed by a one-lap undercut effectively.
- Sector 3: Turns 19–23, pit straight approach. Singapore's pit entry is unusually long, making the undercut gain difficult to reverse once lost. Final braking at Turn 23 before the pit straight is where tire cliff is most visible.

Intervention notes: Singapore is the most physically demanding circuit for drivers. Cockpit temperatures reach 55°C+; driver fatigue from lap 30 onwards reduces braking precision. Monitor lockup frequency in sector 1 as a fatigue/tire condition proxy — a sudden increase in sector 1 lockups after lap 30 is a fatigue or tire cliff signal. Undercut window: open within 2 laps of any competitor in the 4 s window pitting.
