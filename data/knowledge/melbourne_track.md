# Melbourne Track Intelligence
Albert Park is a semi-permanent street circuit around a public lake. The smooth bitumen surface provides low mechanical grip early in the weekend but evolves significantly. Safety car frequency is among the highest on the calendar — almost every race involves at least one intervention. Braking stability into Turns 1, 3, and 9 are the key events.

Key sectors:
- Sector 1: Turn 1 heavy braking from high speed, Turn 2 fast right, Turns 3–4 chicane. Understeer at Turn 3 is the primary front-tire load moment; flat-spotting under ABS is common in the opening stint on cold tires.
- Sector 2: Turns 5–11, park-side section. Medium speed, lower tire load. Turn 9 (Ascari-style chicane) is the key rear lock-up risk under late braking.
- Sector 3: Turns 12–16, lakeside. Turns 13–14 fast chicane; Turn 15 final hairpin. Undercut window at Turn 15 braking is the primary strategy zone; any car within 4 s is vulnerable to undercut within 2 laps of the window opening.

Intervention notes: At Melbourne, track position is often more valuable than tire condition because safety car guarantees (no-cost pit stop) are probable. Do not extend a tire beyond condition if a competitor pits — free tire under safety car is the dominant strategy. Front tire flat-spotting is the most common early race event; if vibration develops above 30 Hz through steering, pit within 2 laps.
