# Montreal Weather Patterns
The Canadian Grand Prix is held in June in Montreal. The weather is famously unpredictable; the circuit has hosted multiple safety car-heavy wet races and mixed condition events. June in Quebec brings warm but highly variable conditions with genuine rain risk on any given race day.

Conditions:
- Wet risk: High. The 2007 and 2011 races were run in wet and drying conditions. Engineers must plan for wet as a primary scenario, not a fallback.
- Temperature: Air 18–27°C, track 28–40°C in dry conditions. Tire thermal degradation is moderate.
- Variability: Montreal weather changes rapidly. A dry morning can precede a wet afternoon within an hour. Monitor radar from the pre-race window.
- Wind: The island circuit is exposed; variable winds 15–30 km/h from the river affect the start/finish complex.

Intermediate tire window: Montreal has one of the wider intermediate tire operating ranges of any circuit — the medium-speed layout with narrow straights means the inters stay competitive for 5–10 laps longer into a drying track than at a high-speed circuit. Holding on intermediates one extra stop cycle can be rewarded if rain threat continues through the race.
