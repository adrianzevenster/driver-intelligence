# Jeddah Weather Patterns
The Saudi Arabian Grand Prix is held at night under floodlights, which significantly affects thermal conditions. Track temperature at race start is approximately 28–32°C in March — warmer than Singapore despite the time of day due to the desert ambient heat retained in the asphalt.

Conditions:
- Dry: Almost always. Jeddah has not experienced a rain-affected race weekend.
- Night race: Track temperature drops 4–6°C through the race duration as ambient cools. Similar to Abu Dhabi in the falling-temperature pattern, this progressively extends tire windows through the second half of the race.
- Humidity: Coastal humidity from the Red Sea (50–65%). Higher humidity than inland Gulf circuits; affects brake cooling marginally.
- Wind: Occasional sea breezes (10–20 km/h). The long main straight can have crosswinds that affect car stability at DRS speeds.

Compound implications: The temperature drop from lap 1 to lap 50 means a tire that looks marginal at lap 25 may stabilise by lap 35. Do not trigger a reactive pit stop based on early wear readings alone — model the temperature trend. The main strategic variable is safety car timing rather than tire degradation.
