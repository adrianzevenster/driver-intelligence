# Baku Track Intelligence
The Baku City Circuit has the longest DRS zone in Formula 1 — the 2.2 km main straight from Turn 16 to Turn 1. The circuit is a hybrid: a high-speed section on the boulevard and a narrow, twisting old-city section (Turns 6–10) requiring precision. Tire wear at Baku is lower than most circuits because of the long straight-line time and relatively few high-load corners. Safety car frequency is high.

Key sectors:
- Sector 1: Turns 1–5, wide boulevard sweeper through Turns 1–3, then downhill into Turn 5. The Turn 1–2 castle sweeper at speed is the defining overtaking zone; brake bias matters on entry.
- Sector 2: Turns 6–10, narrow old city. Turn 8 tightens unpredictably — cars must slow to near 50 km/h. Mechanical grip critical; wall contact risk high. This sector determines track position and safety car frequency.
- Sector 3: Turns 11–16, return to the boulevard. Long acceleration phase from Turn 16 to the main straight start. Rear traction on exit from Turn 16 is the only meaningful rear-wear moment.

Intervention notes: Baku is typically a one-stop race because tire wear is low. A safety car almost always occurs; the optimal strategy often depends on safety car timing. Do not pit on lap 15–20 unless tire condition demands it — wait for the safety car window. If safety car does not arrive, a one-stop with Medium start becomes standard. Grip estimate threshold: 0.65 (lower than most circuits — Baku surface is smooth, reducing expected grip level).
