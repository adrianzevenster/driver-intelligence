# Spa-Francorchamps Track Intelligence
Spa is one of the most demanding circuits for tires and driver energy. The Raidillon-Eau Rouge complex at the top of the hill is taken flat in dry conditions and is the defining commitment point of the lap. Sector 2 through the Hautes Fagnes plateau is where rear and front loads build fastest.

Key sectors:
- Sector 1: La Source hairpin, Eau Rouge, Raidillon. Raidillon exit is high-speed and high-load; front-right and rear-left thermal stress is highest here. A flat-out exit requires confidence in grip; if grip falls below 0.65 in wet conditions, lift marginally.
- Sector 2: Kemmel Straight, Les Combes, Malmedy, Rivage, Pouhon, Fagnes. Pouhon is the key rear-load corner — sustained 4G lateral through a double-apex at speed. Rear degradation indicator is most sensitive here.
- Sector 3: Stavelot, Campus, Blanchimont, Bus Stop chicane. Blanchimont is taken near-flat; rear instability above 280 km/h is a compound degradation signal.

Intervention notes: rear wear rate at Spa is the primary strategy driver. If rear wear exceeds 55% at lap 15 on Medium compound, initiate pit window call. Front-left load is secondary concern compared to rear. The Bus Stop chicane causes brake temperature spikes; monitor front brake fade at end of long stints.
