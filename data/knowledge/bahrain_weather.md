# Bahrain Weather Patterns
The Bahrain Grand Prix is held at night in March. Air temperature typically ranges from 22–28°C at race start (significantly cooler than afternoon practice at 35–40°C). Track temperature at race start is typically 28–35°C and falls further through the race as radiative cooling accelerates. No rain risk.

Conditions:
- Dry: Standard. Only compound at Bahrain is meaningful for strategy — no wet-weather interruptions in recent history.
- Dust and sand: The desert location means sand can be blown onto the circuit by wind. A sudden increase in track temperature differential (track cooler than expected lap times suggest) is an early indicator of sand on the racing line. Monitor grip estimate; if it drops below 0.70 suddenly, sand ingestion is likely.
- Wind: Shamal wind events (northwesterly) occur in spring and can deposit sand rapidly. A wind speed above 25 km/h from the northwest during the race is the trigger condition.

Track evolution: One of the highest track evolution circuits on the calendar. Lap times typically improve by 1.5–2.5 s from the opening lap to lap 15 as the rubber-free surface grips up. Do not compare early stint lap times directly to qualifying pace; adjust mental model for evolution rate.

Temperature gradient effects: The 10°C drop from afternoon to night changes tire behaviour. Softs that overheated in qualifying may be comfortable in the cooler race conditions, extending their stint range by 3–5 laps relative to qualifying simulations.
