# Monaco Track Intelligence
Monaco is a street circuit with the narrowest margins on the calendar. Mechanical grip dominates; aerodynamic downforce is secondary because corner speeds are low. Tire thermal loads are unusually low, but the abrasive concrete-and-asphalt surface can generate unexpected degradation if the driver rides curbs aggressively.

Key sectors:
- Sector 1: Sainte Dévote, Beau Rivage climb, Massenet, Casino Square. High kerb-riding through Casino; front-left takes repeated kerb impact load. Monitor for flat-spotting on cold tires in the opening 3 laps.
- Sector 2: Mirabeau Haute, Mirabeau Bas (Loews hairpin). The Loews hairpin is the tightest corner in F1. Traction out of Loews is the primary rear-wear moment; wheelspin on exit compounds wear rapidly.
- Sector 3: Portier, Tunnel, Nouvelle Chicane, Tabac, Swimming Pool complex, Rascasse, Anthony Noghes. Tunnel exit is high-speed; trust the car blind. Rascasse is a key undercut pivot — any lock-up here induces flat-spot that forces early pit.

Intervention notes: Monaco overtaking is virtually impossible. Any decision to pit must be driven by undercut opportunity within 3 laps of a competitor, not tire condition. If front-left flat-spot develops (typically from Sainte Dévote braking), pit within 2 laps — the vibration builds exponentially and compromises steering at Tabac. Grip threshold for intervention: 0.68 — lower than other circuits due to mechanical nature of grip here.
