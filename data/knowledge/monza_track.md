# Monza Track Intelligence
Monza is a power circuit with long straights and heavy-braking chicanes. Downforce levels are minimal — cars run their flattest wings of the season. Tire thermal load is unusually low because the circuit has few high-speed corners, but braking zones are severe and repeated.

Key sectors:
- Sector 1: Variante del Rettifilo (chicane 1) and Curva Grande. Heavy brake event into the chicane; brake fade builds across the stint.
- Sector 2: Variante della Roggia (chicane 2), Lesmos 1 and 2. Mechanical grip on exit defines rear wear rate. Lesmos reward good rotation but punish excess front load.
- Sector 3: Ascari chicane and Parabolica. Parabolica exit traction is the most lap-time-sensitive point; rear degradation risk if driver applies throttle early on worn rubber.

Intervention notes: brake temperatures are the primary failure mode, not tire wear — if front brake temps exceed 1100°C on entry to Variante del Rettifilo, increase brake-bias toward rear and consider cooling lap. Tire wear rates are typically benign; pit strategy is usually driven by undercut opportunity rather than compound cliff. If rear wear unexpectedly exceeds 45% before lap 25 on Softs, bring forward pit window.
