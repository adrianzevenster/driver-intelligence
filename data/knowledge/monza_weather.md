# Monza Weather Patterns
Monza sits in the Po Valley and is susceptible to late-afternoon thunderstorms in September, which is when the Italian Grand Prix is held. Rain events are typically short but intense and can arrive within a single lap window.

Conditions and tire crossover:
- Dry: Slick compounds standard; Soft typically used for short stints on low-degradation circuit.
- Light rain or damp: Intermediate crossover is aggressive — even 0.2 mm of rain on the main straight reduces aquaplaning risk threshold significantly at 340+ km/h. Consider Intermediates immediately if rain is reported in sector 1.
- Heavy rain: Full wet mandatory. Do not extend slick stint beyond the lap rain is first observed on the straight; at Monza speeds a snap aquaplane can be race-ending.

Track evolution: Monza's surface rubbers in quickly in dry conditions (typically lap 5–8). Later in the session grip improves and lap times naturally fall. Discounting grip-falloff early is a common mistake; instruct driver to trust the car more in the final 10 laps of each stint.

Wind: A headwind on the main straight of more than 15 km/h reduces top speed by 5–8 km/h and slightly decreases rear downforce on the straight. Crosswind at Parabolica can unsettle turn-in; reduce speed commitment if wind exceeds 20 km/h from the west.
