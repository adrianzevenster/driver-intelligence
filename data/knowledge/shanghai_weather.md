# Shanghai Weather Patterns
The Chinese Grand Prix is held in April, making it one of the coolest races on the calendar alongside Melbourne. Track temperatures are typically 18–26°C, which significantly slows tire warm-up and can require an extra lap to bring compounds into operating window.

Conditions:
- Wet risk: Moderate. April in Shanghai carries genuine rain probability; the 2024 race weekend saw both dry and wet conditions. Intermediate tires become relevant more frequently than at other early-season rounds.
- Temperature: Air 15–22°C, track 18–26°C. Compounds warm up slowly; tire blanket temperature retention is critical in the warm-up lap.
- Fog: Early morning practice sessions can see low visibility; this rarely affects the race directly but disrupts data collection.
- Wind: Variable 10–25 km/h crosswinds from the east; can affect DRS stability on the back straight.

Strategy implications: Cool conditions flatten the tire degradation curve — medium compound can last nearly as long as hard at this circuit temperature. Overcut strategy becomes viable because tire temperature drop under safety car conditions is less harmful than at hot circuits; the compound can be revived within a restart lap.
