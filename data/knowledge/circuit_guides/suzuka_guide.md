SUZUKA CIRCUIT — Race Intelligence Guide
Circuit: Suzuka International Racing Course, Suzuka, Japan
Type: Permanent | Laps: 53 | Avg Speed: ~235 kph | Lap Length: 5.807 km

CIRCUIT CHARACTERISTICS
Unique figure-of-eight layout with the overpass at the Crossings section. Demanding mix of ultra-high-speed corners (130R, Spoon Curve), medium-speed chicanes (S1 chicane, Degner), and the iconic Esses sequence. Low to medium downforce level — aerodynamic setup is a compromise between straight-line speed and corner stability. One of the most technical circuits for set-up; balance changes through the lap are significant.

TYRE BEHAVIOUR
Deg level: MEDIUM-HIGH. Rear-dominated wear — 130R (T17), Spoon (T16), and Esses loading cause significant rear lateral wear. Front wear from S1 chicane braking (T3-T4) and Hairpin (T11). Rear-left takes the most punishment. Medium and Hard compounds typical — Soft possible in cooler conditions (April races). 1-stop race is achievable with good pace management. 2-stop faster if temperatures are high or rain intervenes. Graining common in cooler morning sessions, but race is typically drier and warmer. Undercut window: laps 18-23.

SAFETY CAR HISTORY
Probability: MEDIUM (~50%). Typical triggers: S1 chicane kerb strikes causing suspension damage, Spoon curve barrier contacts, pit lane accidents. Weather-induced SC very common — sudden rain squalls can arrive mid-race in April. VSC more common than full SC for debris clearance. Lap 1 incidents at T1 (hairpin after long run from grid) are occasional.

FAILURE MODES
Tyres: High-speed rear loading most important failure mode. 130R at near-300 kph means any tyre degradation translates to a high-speed spin risk — agents should escalate rear wear warnings. Brakes: Moderate wear at T1, T11 hairpin. Cooling: High-downforce Esses loading builds heat in rear suspension and diffuser. PU: High-speed sections require sustained power — no significant fuel saving opportunity in S1/S2. ERS harvest good at hairpin and Chicane braking. Bodywork: Kerb strikes at S1 chicane regularly cause floor/diffuser damage.

STRATEGY PATTERNS
Pit lane time loss: ~23 seconds. Track position important — overtaking possible on the main straight but otherwise difficult. Undercut effective from P5 downward. Safety car intervention heavily rewarded — free stop opportunity should always be taken given high tyre deg. If rain arrives mid-race intermediate strategy can gain or lose multiple positions.

WEATHER
Highly variable. April Japanese GP: rain probability ~40%. Fog possible in morning sessions (not race). Track temperature: 10-25°C depending on conditions. Cold tyres take longer to come in on out-lap after SC. 2026 race in late March/early April — cool and wet conditions most likely.

KEY CORNERS FOR AGENT ALERTS
T1 (hairpin): Lap 1 lockup risk. High brake temp on cold tyres.
Esses (T4-T10): Rear stability critical — any tyre degradation telegraphed here first.
130R (T17): Highest-risk corner for rear blowout / tyre failure on worn rubber. Agent should flag rear wear >0.70 here as critical.
Chicane (T18-T19): Brake fade and lockup risk, kerb strikes cause floor damage.
Spoon (T16): Rear-left loading — primary degradation driver for rear compounds.
