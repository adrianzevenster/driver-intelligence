BAHRAIN INTERNATIONAL CIRCUIT — Race Intelligence Guide
Circuit: Bahrain International Circuit, Sakhir, Bahrain
Type: Permanent | Laps: 57 | Avg Speed: ~210 kph | Lap Length: 5.412 km

CIRCUIT CHARACTERISTICS
Clockwise layout with 15 corners. Three long straights connected by tight hairpins and medium-speed complexes. The desert location means abrasive tarmac and significant sand/dust contamination off the racing line. Track rubbering-in across the weekend significantly improves grip but the off-line surface remains treacherous.

TYRE BEHAVIOUR
Rear-left dominant wear circuit due to clockwise direction and high-load right-handers (T11-T13 complex). Deg level: HIGH. Medium and Hard compounds are standard — Soft is viable for qualifying only. Rear-left graining common in the first stint if starting on Mediums in cool track conditions (evening race). Front wear at T1, T8 braking zones. Typical strategy: 2-stop (Medium–Hard–Hard) or aggressive 1-stop (Hard–Hard) for top qualifiers. Undercut window opens around lap 18-22 for the first stop.

SAFETY CAR HISTORY
Probability: MEDIUM (~55% deployment rate historically). Common triggers: T1 first-lap contact, T4 barrier strikes, T10 overtaking incidents. SC typically deployed laps 1-5 or mid-race after pit stop traffic incidents. Virtual SC common for debris retrieval.

FAILURE MODES
ERS/Power Unit: Very high electrical demand — final sector has multiple traction zones requiring full ERS deployment. Battery thermal stress elevated due to ambient temperatures (25-35°C track temp at race start). Brake wear: moderate-high at T1, T8, T11 — teams run medium-high brake duct settings. Sand ingestion risk if car goes off-track — radiator blockage possible. Overheating risk for cars running in dirty air through the tight infield.

STRATEGY PATTERNS
Pit lane time loss: ~22 seconds. Undercut is effective due to significant lap time loss from tyre deg. Teams routinely undercut rivals from P4-P8. Overcut less viable given track position importance at T1. Safety car restarts heavily favour cars on newer tyres.

WEATHER
Dry race probability: ~90%. Evening races (race start 18:00 local) mean track temperatures drop through the race — improving grip on Hards late on. Wind can shift — headwind on the main straight affects braking points. Occasional sand storms in the days before the race affect track preparation. Ambient temp: 20-28°C at race time. Rain probability: under 5%.

KEY CORNERS FOR AGENT ALERTS
T1 (hairpin): High lockup probability on lap 1 and after SC restarts. High brake temp spike.
T8 (hairpin): Second major braking zone — rear locking common on worn Mediums.
T11-T13 (complex): Rear-left loading — primary driver of RR/RL deg and thermal deg alerts.
T15 (final chicane): Front wing damage risk on kerb strikes.
