SHANGHAI INTERNATIONAL CIRCUIT — Race Intelligence Guide
Circuit: Shanghai International Circuit, Shanghai, China
Type: Permanent | Laps: 56 | Avg Speed: ~215 kph | Lap Length: 5.451 km

CIRCUIT CHARACTERISTICS
Distinctive Hermann Tilke design with the unique hairpin-into-straight layout at T1-T6 — a long decreasing-radius corner that places sustained loading on the rear tyres. Back straight (S2) is one of the longer DRS zones on the calendar. S3 is technical medium-speed. High downforce setup. Track surface is medium-grip asphalt with good rubber from multiple events including Sprint weekends. Layout favours strong traction and straight-line speed. Sprint format in 2026 — race strategy compressed by the short format.

TYRE BEHAVIOUR
Deg level: MEDIUM. Rear-dominated — T1-T6 complex (long constant-radius left sweeper from T3) loads the rear-right heavily. T13 hairpin is the primary braking zone and front wear point. Medium and Hard compounds typical for the main race. Sprint format means tyre allocation is shared — strategic choices more constrained than a standard weekend. 2-stop race typical for 56 laps. 1-stop viable if rear temps stay in range. Undercut window: laps 18-24.

SAFETY CAR HISTORY
Probability: MEDIUM (~50%). T1 complex is a common contact zone — the decreasing radius catches drivers off-guard on lap 1. T13-T14 hairpin sequence produces braking incidents. Changeable weather (rain arriving mid-race) triggers SC periods for Intermediate tyre changes. VSC frequent for debris from suspension damage on kerbs.

FAILURE MODES
Rear tyres: T1-T6 complex is a sustained high-load rear-right loading section — arguably the single most severe sustained rear loading on the calendar. Rear-right thermal deg and blistering are the primary tyre failure mode. Agent should flag RR deg early and aggressively. Brakes: T13 hairpin requires very heavy braking from 290 kph. High brake wear at this corner — teams run high brake duct settings despite the cooler temperatures. PU/ERS: Long back straight demands full power — battery state critical entering S2. T14 (slow hairpin exit) provides ERS harvest opportunity. Cooling: April temperatures (12-22°C ambient) are cool — not a cooling-limited circuit, but sudden rain can drastically change thermal management for slick tyres.

STRATEGY PATTERNS
Pit lane time loss: ~24 seconds. Back straight DRS zone creates significant overtaking opportunity — track position slightly less dominant than Hungary. Undercut effective from P4-P8. Sprint format 2026: main race still full distance (56 laps), Sprint is separate (19 laps). Sprint tyre allocation constraint means teams must manage tyre sets carefully across both events. SC scenario: critical to manage RR deg heading into SC periods — if RR is already borderline, pit immediately under SC.

WEATHER
April weather is changeable — rain probability: ~35%. Cold and wet conditions are common in April Shanghai, including in race conditions. Intermediate tyres on a cold track surface have unusual behaviour — warm-up phase is longer. Track temperature: 15-30°C depending on conditions. If rain arrives, intermediate compound is typically the faster choice within 2 laps of rainfall. Teams with strong wet setups (high-downforce bias) gain most.

KEY CORNERS FOR AGENT ALERTS
T1-T6 (long decreasing-radius complex): Rear-right primary loading zone. Sustained high G over 8+ seconds. Most important corner for tyre deg monitoring.
T8 (hairpin at the bottom of the back straight): Lockup risk — very fast approach from the T6 exit. Brake temps elevated.
T13-T14 (hairpin): Heaviest braking zone on lap. Lockup and rear lock risk on worn Mediums. Primary contact zone.
T16 (final chicane): Kerb strikes cause diffuser damage. Front wing scrape risk.
