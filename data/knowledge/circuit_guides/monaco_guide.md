CIRCUIT DE MONACO — Race Intelligence Guide
Circuit: Circuit de Monaco, Monte Carlo, Monaco
Type: Street / Temporary | Laps: 78 | Avg Speed: ~140 kph | Lap Length: 3.337 km

CIRCUIT CHARACTERISTICS
The most iconic circuit in motorsport. Slowest average speed on the calendar (~140 kph). Lined entirely by barriers with zero run-off — contact ends races immediately. Narrow, twisty layout through the streets of Monte Carlo. Extreme track evolution — green and very low grip at the start of practice. Unique: the circuit reverses direction through the tunnel (T10-T12). Qualifying is disproportionately important — overtaking on track is nearly impossible. Strategy and SC timing are the primary variables that change race outcomes.

TYRE BEHAVIOUR
Deg level: LOW. The slowest circuit means the lowest thermal loading on tyres. Compounds last much longer than at any other venue. This paradoxically makes strategy harder: a 1-stop race (Hard at the start, no stop required physically) is possible if track position is maintained. However, overcut is useless — being on fresh tyres doesn't help if you can't pass on track. Soft and Medium are frequently used for the race because pace matters more than longevity when the strategy is track-position based. The key tyre event: a SC that opens the pit window, allowing a free tyre change without losing position to a pit stop delta.

SAFETY CAR HISTORY
Probability: VERY HIGH (~85%). The highest SC probability on the calendar. Any incident ends at a barrier — there is no run-off. Main triggers: swimming pool chicane (T15-T16), Rascasse (T18), T1 (Sainte Devote), and pit lane entry. Multiple SC periods per race are normal. Red flag probability elevated — incidents in the tunnel or at Rascasse are difficult to recover quickly. VSC also common for debris removal. Teams that nail SC timing win Monaco — it is the most important strategic variable by a wide margin.

FAILURE MODES
Front wing: Very high risk of front wing damage from contact with barriers at the chicane (T15-T16), swimming pool entry, and Rascasse. Front wing damage typically ends competitive race pace immediately. Pit lane: Very long pit lane (unusually high time loss ~29-31 seconds) and narrow entry — pit lane incidents including front wing damage on the wall or contact with mechanics are possible. ERS/Battery: Ironically, ERS harvesting is very low at Monaco due to minimal braking energy. Battery depletion under high electrical demand (DRS, motor use) can be a concern. Teams run high-efficiency modes. Suspension: Kerb strikes through the chicane and swimming pool put high impact loads on suspension. Water drains in the road surface add bumps. Gearbox: High gear change count due to the constant acceleration-braking pattern — gearbox reliability is monitored closely.

STRATEGY PATTERNS
Pit lane time loss: ~29-31 seconds (longest on calendar). This extreme pit loss time means pitting voluntarily (outside of SC) costs massive track position that cannot be recovered. Strategy is entirely SC-dependent. Under green: hold position, do not pit unless tyre failure is imminent. Under SC: all cars on suboptimal tyres must pit immediately — the window is free. 1-stop race (Hard-Hard with late SC stop) or 0-stop (if SC never comes) is theoretically optimal. Undercut: irrelevant — track position cannot be recovered. Overcut: irrelevant — no pace differential on clear air laps matters if you can't pass.

WEATHER
May weather is mild but variable. Rain probability: ~35%. Rain changes Monaco completely — wet tyre warm-up is slow through the tunnel and on the long straight sections, but the intermediate is the critical tyre here. Cold concrete surface at Monaco means wet tyres take longer to come in vs other venues. If rain arrives, the SC will be deployed — use it for a free stop. Ambient: 18-24°C. Track temperature: 28-40°C race day. Night sessions are cool.

KEY CORNERS FOR AGENT ALERTS
T1 (Sainte Devote): Lap 1 contact probability very high. Cold tyres + SC restart incidents.
T5 (Casino Square): High-speed right through a narrow section — barrier on both sides.
T10-T12 (Tunnel): Vision change from bright to dark — unusual cornering event. Exit is very fast.
T15-T16 (Chicane / Nouvelle Chicane): Primary front wing damage zone. Lockup risk. SC trigger zone.
T18 (Rascasse): The slowest corner on the circuit — parking risk, SC trigger for any incident.
T19 (Anthony Noghes): Final corner, entry into the pit straight. Understeer pushes to the wall.
