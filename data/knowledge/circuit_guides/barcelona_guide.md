CIRCUIT DE CATALUNYA — Race Intelligence Guide
Circuit: Circuit de Catalunya, Montmelo, Barcelona, Spain
Type: Permanent | Laps: 66 | Avg Speed: ~210 kph | Lap Length: 4.657 km

CIRCUIT CHARACTERISTICS
High-downforce circuit used extensively for pre-season testing — teams know it better than any other venue. Mix of high-speed S1 corners (T3, T9), medium-speed complex (T5-T6), and technical hairpins (T10, T14). Long back straight with DRS zone. Asphalt surface well-rubbered from testing and GP use. High mechanical grip compared to street circuits. One of the most complete circuits for chassis development.

TYRE BEHAVIOUR
Deg level: MEDIUM-HIGH. Front-dominated wear — T3 (high-speed right-hander taken at 270 kph) and T9 load fronts heavily. Rear deg from T5 traction zone and T14 chicane. Wind at T10 can shift handling balance unexpectedly, increasing tyre loading. Medium and Hard compounds typical. Soft is viable for short stints but degrades quickly in warm conditions. Standard race: 2-stop (Soft-Medium-Hard or Medium-Medium-Hard). 1-stop viable only in low-degradation cooler conditions. Undercut window: laps 18-25 for first stop.

SAFETY CAR HISTORY
Probability: LOW (~35%). One of the safest circuits — wide run-off areas, experienced drivers know the limits well. Main SC triggers: lap 1 T1 incidents, T10 contact under braking. VSC more common than full SC. Red flag very rare.

FAILURE MODES
Fronts: Highest front loading on calendar at T3 — thermal deg of fronts can occur rapidly in hot conditions (30°C+). Agent should monitor front temp closely in the first 15 laps. Brakes: Moderate wear — T1 and T10 are primary braking zones. Long pit lane delta reduces incentive to stop early. Cooling: May temperatures (22-28°C ambient) are manageable, but radiator settings are conservative due to sustained high-speed running. PU: Sustained high throttle on the back straight — ERS fully deployed here. T9 chicane recovery zone important for battery state. Wind: T10 is exposed — crosswind causes oversteer mid-corner, resulting in increased rear tyre wear.

STRATEGY PATTERNS
Pit lane time loss: ~25 seconds (longest pit lane on calendar). Overcut is viable here specifically because the high pit loss time means a driver can push on old tyres and gain time vs a car that has just pitted. 2-stop is standard; aggressive 1-stop with Hard tyres from qualifying is occasionally successful. Safety car is a rare bonus — teams on alternate strategies benefit from any VSC/SC. Barcelona is a classic undercut venue from P4-P7 on the road.

WEATHER
Dry race probability: ~85%. May: 18-28°C ambient. Occasional thunderstorms possible but forecast is usually reliable. Wind direction shifts through the day — the T10 crosswind is variable and affects set-up choice. Track surface temperature: 35-50°C race day.

KEY CORNERS FOR AGENT ALERTS
T3 (Repsol, high-speed right): Primary front-tyre loading corner. Front thermal deg early warning here first.
T5-T6 (medium complex): Rear traction loading — rear deg visible in sector 2.
T9 (last corner of S1, chicane): Front wear from braking, understeer from front deg.
T10 (hairpin): Main braking zone — lockup risk on lap 1 and under pressure. Wind effect adds oversteer risk on exit.
T14 (final chicane): Kerb strikes cause floor damage. Front wing contact risk.
