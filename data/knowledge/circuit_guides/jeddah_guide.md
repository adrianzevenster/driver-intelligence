JEDDAH CORNICHE CIRCUIT — Race Intelligence Guide
Circuit: Jeddah Corniche Circuit, Jeddah, Saudi Arabia
Type: Street / Temporary | Laps: 50 | Avg Speed: ~250 kph | Lap Length: 6.174 km

CIRCUIT CHARACTERISTICS
Fastest street circuit on the calendar. High-speed walls with minimal run-off — one of the most dangerous layouts in terms of barrier proximity. 27 corners, many taken flat or near-flat. Concrete surface offers low mechanical grip compared to asphalt permanent tracks. Track evolution is dramatic across the weekend as rubber is laid down on a surface used only once per year. Night race — track temperatures lower than ambient.

TYRE BEHAVIOUR
Deg level: LOW-MEDIUM. Surprisingly gentle on tyres given the speed — long straights mean lower lateral loading than a circuit like Bahrain. Front wear dominated by T1 and T27 braking. Rear wear from high-speed exits (T22-T26 complex). Soft compound viable for race in cool night conditions — faster teams often use Soft-Medium or Soft-Hard. Graining risk on Softs in the first 5 laps if track not fully rubbered-in. 1-stop race typical. Undercut window: laps 15-20.

SAFETY CAR HISTORY
Probability: VERY HIGH (~80%+ deployment rate). Lap 1 incidents almost guaranteed at T1 (high-speed compression, poor visibility). Mid-race SC from wall strikes in the S2 high-speed complex common. Red flag probability elevated — concrete walls leave minimal recovery space. Multiple SC periods in one race are common. Teams should always plan a SC scenario strategy.

FAILURE MODES
Brakes: Very high brake temps through S2 — T11, T13, T17 sequence requires repeated heavy braking from 280+ kph. Teams run maximum brake duct cooling. Power Unit: Full throttle for ~68% of lap — highest PU stress on calendar. ERS heavily loaded, especially the long DRS straights. Battery depletion risk if ERS management is lost. Cooling: Despite being a night race, wall proximity limits airflow — overheating concern for slower cars stuck in traffic. Barrier strikes: chassis and suspension damage risk at any wall contact given the speed differentials.

STRATEGY PATTERNS
Pit lane time loss: ~24 seconds. 1-stop (Soft-Hard or Medium-Hard) is standard. SC effectively resets the race — teams on alternate strategies benefit most. If running SC, tyre delta between Soft and Hard is large — overcut on Hard is risky. Night conditions mean tyre warm-up takes longer after SC restart.

WEATHER
Dry probability: ~95%. Night race temps: 22-28°C ambient, track 28-35°C. No rain expected. Wind funnelled through the street circuit can cause handling shifts at high-speed corners — particularly the T22-T26 sequence where lateral wind generates understeer.

KEY CORNERS FOR AGENT ALERTS
T1 (high-speed compression): Highest incident probability on lap 1. Brake temps spike immediately.
T12-T13 (chicane): Lockup risk — tight entry after 280 kph straight. High brake fade risk on worn tyres.
T22-T26 (high-speed complex): Rear instability risk — any oversteer here is into a concrete wall.
T27 (final hairpin): Brake wear hotspot, contact risk on overtaking attempts.
