MIAMI INTERNATIONAL AUTODROME — Race Intelligence Guide
Circuit: Miami International Autodrome, Miami Gardens, Florida
Type: Street / Semi-Permanent | Laps: 57 | Avg Speed: ~220 kph | Lap Length: 5.412 km

CIRCUIT CHARACTERISTICS
Built around the Hard Rock Stadium complex. Mix of high-speed sweeps, tight chicanes, and slow hairpins. Surface is highly abrasive — artificially textured tarmac laid over car park surface. Significant track evolution across the weekend but grip levels remain lower than established permanent venues. Bumpy surface, especially over kerbs, increases tyre loading variability. Off-line surface is particularly contaminated with marbles by mid-race.

TYRE BEHAVIOUR
Deg level: HIGH. One of the highest degradation circuits on the calendar due to surface abrasion and thermal loading. Rear-dominated wear — T14-T16 complex (slow hairpin sequence) causes sustained rear lateral loading. Front wear elevated at T1 (heavy braking), T11, and T17 hairpin. Graining common on Mediums in the first 6-8 laps in hot conditions. Soft compound typically qualifying-only. Medium-Hard 2-stop is the default race strategy. 1-stop Hard-Hard sometimes viable for cars with clean air. Undercut is strong — pit lane position shortens the loss time. Undercut window: laps 15-22.

SAFETY CAR HISTORY
Probability: HIGH (~70%). T1 first-lap incidents are frequent given the 90-degree right-hander after a long straight. Bumpy surface through T6-T9 causes car stability issues and occasional barrier contact. T14-T16 chicane produces lockups and subsequent penalties that can trigger caution periods. Mid-race SC common from backmarker incidents at wall sections. Red flag potential if debris is on the high-speed sections.

FAILURE MODES
Tyres: Abrasive surface accelerates wear — thermal deg shows earlier than at comparable circuits. Agent should lower warning thresholds for tyre deg vs Bahrain norms. Brakes: Moderate-high — T1, T11, T17 require heavy braking. Brake temps elevated by high ambient and track temperatures (45-55°C track temp typical). Cooling: Heat and humidity (30-35°C ambient, 60-80% humidity) stress all cooling systems. ERS thermal management critical — cars frequently hit power limits in traffic. Suspension: Bumpy surface increases suspension loading — a common failure mode in Miami history.

STRATEGY PATTERNS
Pit lane time loss: ~21 seconds (short pit lane). Strong undercut due to high deg and relatively short pit loss time. Teams typically undercut from P3-P10. 2-stop race with Medium-Medium-Hard or Soft-Medium-Hard. SC scenario: any car on a Medium past lap 30 should strongly consider pitting immediately. Overtaking possible on the back straight (DRS zone) but T1 braking is the primary passing point.

WEATHER
High heat and humidity standard. Rain probability: ~25% (afternoon thunderstorms possible in May). Sudden heavy rain can arrive rapidly — teams monitor radar closely. If rain arrives on lap 20+, Intermediate tyres can gain significant time. Track temperature: 45-58°C. High humidity reduces ERS cooling efficiency. Wind direction variable — affects T10 high-speed complex handling.

KEY CORNERS FOR AGENT ALERTS
T1 (right-angle braking zone): Lap 1 lockup probability very high. High brake temp immediately.
T6-T8 (chicane complex): Bumpy surface — understeer and suspension stress. Kerb strikes common.
T14-T16 (low-speed chicane): Primary lockup zone mid-race. Brake fade risk on worn compounds. Rear tyre damage from kicking kerbs.
T17 (hairpin): Front wing damage risk from kerb strikes. Significant brake wear zone.
