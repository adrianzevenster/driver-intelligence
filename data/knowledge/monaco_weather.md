# Monaco Weather Patterns
Monaco is held in May when Mediterranean weather is typically stable, but the street circuit's proximity to the sea and the hills above the circuit create localised shower risk. Rain at Monaco is the most consequential event in F1 — Safety Car deployment is nearly guaranteed, and the circuit becomes very difficult to drive in wet conditions.

Tire crossover thresholds:
- First hint of rain on the circuit surface: pit for Intermediates within 1 lap. There is no grace period at Monaco — the tightness of the circuit and low speeds mean aquaplaning risk is lower, but loss of mechanical grip through Casino and Tunnel exit is immediate and severe.
- Drying track: intermediates are viable for longer at Monaco than other circuits because a dry line forms slowly on the narrow surface. Do not switch to slicks until at least 4 laps after rain stops and the track temp is above 30°C.
- Full wet threshold: sustained heavy rain with standing water in the Swimming Pool chicane or at Rascasse. Full Wet tires mandatory.

Safety Car probability: Monaco has the highest Safety Car probability on the calendar. Rain virtually guarantees deployment. Strategy should assume at least one Safety Car intervention in wet conditions and plan tire windows around it.

Track evolution: Monaco's surface does not rubber in as dramatically as conventional circuits. Grip improvement through the race is modest (~0.5 s over 50 laps versus 1.5–2 s elsewhere). Do not rely on track evolution to extend stints beyond compound capability.
