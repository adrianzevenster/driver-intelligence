# Las Vegas Street Circuit — Track Characteristics
The Las Vegas Street Circuit is the fastest street circuit on the calendar and one of the longest straights of any F1 race. The layout runs through the Las Vegas Strip and back streets, featuring an extremely long main straight (approximately 1.8 km), two shorter straights, and three primary braking zones. The circuit was added in 2023 and has distinctive characteristics driven by cold desert night temperatures.

Key sectors:
- Sector 1: Start of main straight to Turn 1 (Caesars Palace); maximum speed over 340 km/h on the Strip; Turn 1 is the hardest braking zone on the calendar at this entry speed; first-lap proximity risk in the dark
- Sector 2: Technical section through the casino district backstreets; Turns 4–11; moderate speed; narrow between barriers; mechanical grip domain
- Sector 3: Back straight DRS zone; Turns 12–14 chicane complex; emerges onto final straight back to the Strip

Tire behaviour:
- Cold night temperatures (5–12°C in November) are the dominant variable. Tire warm-up takes multiple laps; cold-ambient temperature tire cliff is different in character from thermal degradation at hot circuits.
- The soft compound struggles to reach operating temperature in cold conditions; engineers must monitor tire temperature closely in the first stint.
- Low-degradation circuit due to smooth asphalt; tires run mechanically clean. One-stop hard strategy is viable.
- Rear tires are exposed at Turn 1 traction exit from very low speed after the hardest braking point.

Visibility and safety: Night race; cockpit visibility limited; driver visibility changes as racing line develops and rubber is laid down. Safety car probability is elevated in the first few laps.
