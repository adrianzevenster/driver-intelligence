# Lusail International Circuit (Qatar) — Track Characteristics
The Lusail International Circuit is a purpose-built motorcycle-turned-Formula-1 circuit in Qatar. It features a high-speed, flowing layout with no long straights and continuous high-load corners that generate extreme tire thermal stress — particularly on the rear axle. The circuit has a very high sustained lateral-force profile that places it among the most demanding tire circuits on the calendar.

Key sectors:
- Sector 1: Turns 1–6; high-speed flowing section; fast direction changes; high sustained rear lateral load
- Sector 2: Turns 7–11; continuation of the flowing medium-high speed layout; rear tire temperature builds progressively
- Sector 3: Turns 12–16; final sector includes Turn 14 heavy braking; main straight DRS approach; Turn 16 fast exit

Tire behaviour:
- Rear tire thermal degradation is the primary challenge. The continuous high-speed corners build rear tire surface temperature faster than almost any other circuit.
- Rear blistering is a known risk at Lusail; monitor rear tire temperature closely from lap 5 onwards.
- Soft compound is unusable beyond 8–10 laps at race conditions; medium is the standard race compound. Hard tire thermal performance at this circuit is strong.
- Sprint race format: Qatar has hosted sprint weekends; tire allocation and strategy differ significantly from standard race weekends.

Circuit pace: Average race speed is approximately 235 km/h due to the flowing layout. Despite the relatively modest top speed, the continuous lateral loads across 57 laps make it one of the most physically demanding circuits for tires.
