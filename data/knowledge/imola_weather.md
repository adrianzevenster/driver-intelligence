# Imola Weather Patterns
The Emilia Romagna Grand Prix is held in May in northern Italy. The circuit sits in the Po Valley, which is prone to significant rainfall events. The 2020 Imola race was held in genuinely wet conditions; the 2023 race weekend was cancelled due to flooding. Rain is not a marginal risk here — it is a genuine strategic factor that must be planned for.

Conditions:
- Wet risk: High by European standards. May in the Po Valley brings frequent convective rain events. Engineers should have a wet strategy as Plan A, not contingency.
- Temperature: Typically 16–24°C air, 22–35°C track. Cooler than summer European rounds; medium compound warm-up time can be extended in low temperatures.
- Variable conditions: Morning and evening rain with afternoon clearing is a common pattern. Track conditions can change within a session.

Strategy implications: A sudden rain shower at Imola forces immediate tactical decisions because the circuit is narrow and overtaking on a drying track is very difficult. Switching to intermediates early to use the grip advantage before the racing line dries is more valuable here than at wider circuits. If it starts raining, being on slicks a lap longer than rivals is less dangerous than normal because passing back is nearly impossible.
