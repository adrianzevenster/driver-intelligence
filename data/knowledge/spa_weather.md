# Spa-Francorchamps Weather Patterns
Spa is famous for hyper-local weather. It is entirely possible for it to be dry at La Source, raining at Raidillon, and dry again at the Bus Stop on the same lap. This is the most strategically complex weather circuit on the calendar.

Microclimate zones:
- La Source / pit straight: Lower altitude, typically driest section. Often last to receive rain.
- Raidillon / Fagnes plateau: Higher altitude, catches rain first. The most dangerous section in wet conditions; Raidillon flat commitment must be abandoned at first hint of damp.
- Pouhon and Stavelot: Sheltered valley sections; drying faster than the plateau.

Tire crossover thresholds:
- If rain is reported at any sector but not all: Intermediate is almost always the correct call, not Full Wet. Slicks on a damp plateau are race-ending risk.
- Full Wet threshold: rainfall > 1.5 mm/lap equivalent or if standing water is reported at Raidillon. Full Wet is faster than Intermediate in genuine standing water on the straight.
- Drying track: switch from Full Wet to Intermediate when sector 1 laptimes on Full Wet are more than 8 s off dry pace AND the Safety Car is not deployed. Switch from Intermediate to Slick when grip estimate exceeds 0.80 and track temp is rising.

Track evolution: rubber laid down early in the weekend is washed away by rain and the circuit reverts toward an unrubbered state. Lap times at the start of the race on a freshly rained-on track will be 2–4 s slower than the equivalent lap 10.

Wind: Crosswind at Kemmel Straight can cause high-speed instability above 310 km/h. Alert driver if wind exceeds 25 km/h perpendicular to the straight; DRS may be unsafe.
