# Jeddah Corniche Circuit — Track Characteristics
The Jeddah Corniche Circuit is the second-fastest street circuit on the calendar, featuring high-speed sweeping corners and the longest continuous DRS zone in F1 (approximately 1.9 km on the main straight). Average race speed exceeds 250 km/h. The circuit runs along the Red Sea waterfront and has very little room for error — the walls are extremely close throughout.

Key sectors:
- Sector 1: sequence of quick sweeping changes of direction; committed entry lines required; high downforce corners
- Sector 2: tight hairpins requiring heavy braking and late apex entries; main opportunity for overtaking
- Sector 3: long main straight with DRS; reaches over 320 km/h at the end; then Turn 27 hard left into the chicane complex

Tire behaviour:
- Low to medium tire degradation for a street circuit; relatively smooth surface by street standards
- Front left shoulder is the most loaded axle through the sweeping sector 1 complex
- Rear tires are heavily loaded in sector 2 traction zones from low-speed hairpins
- Soft compound is viable in qualifying and early stints; medium and hard carry the race

Overtaking:
- Primary overtaking zone: Turn 1 following the main straight DRS
- Secondary: Turn 4 heavy braking
- Safety car risk is high due to the walls and narrow corridors; virtual and full safety cars are frequent at Jeddah

Pit window strategy: Usually a one-stop medium → hard race, with soft undercut attempted on lap 15–20 in typical safety car conditions. The safety car probability is high enough to warrant having a defensive in-lap plan ready from lap 10.
