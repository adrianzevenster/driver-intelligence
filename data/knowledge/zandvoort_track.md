# Circuit Zandvoort — Track Characteristics
Zandvoort is a narrow, high-speed circuit in the Dutch dunes with no meaningful overtaking opportunities outside of pit stop strategy. Its defining feature is the banked final turn (Arie Luyendijk — Turn 14) at 19 degrees of banking, which eliminates the need to slow for the final corner and delivers cars onto the main straight at full speed. The circuit is compact (4.259 km) and technical; qualifying is paramount.

Key sectors:
- Sector 1: Tarzanbocht (Tarzan) hairpin at Turn 1 is the one genuine heavy braking zone; the only overtaking point; then fast flowing Sector 1 exits
- Sector 2: Sustained medium-high speed through the Hugenholtz complex; banked Turns 8–9 (Hugenholtzbocht); high lateral loads on the rear
- Sector 3: Banked Arie Luyendijk corner (Turn 14) allows full throttle; drivers carry significantly more speed than an unbanked circuit; rear stability is crucial

Tire behaviour:
- High rear tire wear from the banked corners; the sustained lateral load through Turns 8–9 and Turn 14 builds heat in the rear axle.
- Front tires are stressed by Turn 1 hard braking and the return sequence in sector 1.
- Two-stop strategies are common because tire life is shortened by the banked corner loads.
- Overcut opportunities at Zandvoort are limited; the undercut is the primary strategic tool because overtaking on track is nearly impossible.

Wind effect: Zandvoort sits in coastal dunes; strong side winds are common. Wind can change the aerodynamic balance lap-to-lap and cause unexpected understeer/oversteer transitions through the banked turns. Monitor wind direction and speed; adjust bias recommendations accordingly.
