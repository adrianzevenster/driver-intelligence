# Zandvoort Weather Patterns
The Dutch Grand Prix is held in late August on the North Sea coast. Zandvoort's coastal dune location makes it one of the most wind-affected circuits on the calendar, and rain is genuinely common in late Dutch summer.

Conditions:
- Wet risk: Moderate to High. Late August in the Netherlands brings unpredictable rain; the 2021 return race weekend saw both wet and dry conditions. Engineers should plan for intermediate conditions.
- Wind: This is the defining variable. North Sea winds consistently reach 25–40 km/h, sometimes gusting higher. The direction varies; engineers must monitor whether winds are from the coast (affecting the start/finish complex) or from inland (affecting the back straight). Strong crosswinds through the banked turns can create snap oversteer risk at the banking exit.
- Temperature: Air 16–23°C, track 22–35°C. Relatively cool for August; tire warm-up is a factor, particularly on safety car restart laps.
- Humidity: Coastal moisture means 60–75% humidity; not extreme but adds to cockpit discomfort.

Wind strategy implication: At circuits where wind direction changes through the race, the optimal racing line through the banked Turn 14 may shift. Drivers should report corner feel changes to the engineer; a car that was balanced in the morning may understeer or oversteer at the same corner as the wind rotates.
