# Baku Weather Patterns
The Azerbaijan Grand Prix in April–June is typically warm and dry (22–30°C). The main weather risk is wind — Baku is exposed on the Caspian coast and gusts of 30–40 km/h have disrupted qualifying and affected race strategy. Rain is rare.

Conditions:
- Dry: Standard. Cool (below 25°C) conditions improve tire wear and extend compound windows.
- Wind: Caspian coastal winds are the primary weather variable. Crosswind on the main straight above 25 km/h creates DRS stability concerns — some teams disable DRS above wind threshold. Inform driver if wind is gusty; Turn 8 in old city with a crosswind gust is a primary crash risk.
- Rain: Extremely rare at Baku. If rain occurs, the street surface becomes very slippery immediately (low rubber, smooth asphalt). Intermediate crossover is immediate — do not attempt to extend slick stint even one lap once rain reaches the main straight.

Safety car: Baku consistently produces at least one safety car or red flag per race due to the old-city sector complexity. Strategy should treat safety car as near-certain and plan pit windows around that assumption.

Track evolution: Baku does not evolve as significantly as other circuits because the smooth surface is inherently lower-grip. The improvement from lap 1 to lap 20 is typically 0.8–1.2 s, less than most circuits. Do not rely on track improvement to bail out a weak starting tire choice.
