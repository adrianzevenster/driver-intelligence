# Monza ERS Deployment
Monza is the highest-energy ERS track on the calendar. Three DRS detection zones and long flat-out sections mean battery is depleted rapidly if deployed aggressively on every straight.

Key deployment zones:
- Main straight (pit straight): Full deployment from Parabolica exit through Variante del Rettifilo braking. Highest absolute speed gain.
- Back straight (Curva Grande to Variante della Roggia): Secondary deployment; typically harvest-recover balance rather than full push.
- Ascari to Parabolica: Deployment from Ascari exit to Parabolica entry adds 0.2–0.3 s per lap when battery is above 0.35.

Battery management thresholds:
- SOC below 0.20 entering the final 15 laps: switch to harvest-priority mapping on the back straight. Loss on back straight saves battery for Parabolica exit and main straight, net neutral or positive.
- SOC below 0.10: abandon deployment mode, full harvest. Alert engineer.
- Avoid deploying during braking phases; brake-regen is the primary recovery source at Monza.

Intervention notes: Monza rewards consistent deployment scheduling. Drivers who over-push battery in the opening stint often face a 15-lap period of reduced power in the final third of a long stint.
