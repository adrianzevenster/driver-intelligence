# Miami International Autodrome — Track Characteristics
The Miami International Autodrome is a purpose-built street circuit surrounding the Hard Rock Stadium. It is a medium-speed layout with a variety of corner types: a high-speed opening sweeper, several tight hairpins, and a fast final sector. The circuit lacks the natural character of permanent tracks but provides multiple DRS zones and overtaking opportunities.

Key sectors:
- Sector 1: Turns 1–5; fast flowing entry then tightens into Turn 4 hairpin; requires stable rear end
- Sector 2: Turns 6–11; slow technical section; tight turns 6 and 8 require late apex and strong traction; high rear tire wear zone
- Sector 3: Turns 12–19; medium-speed flowing segment with back straight DRS; Turn 17–19 chicane exits onto main straight

Tire behaviour:
- Moderate front wear from the mid-speed technical corners in sectors 1 and 2.
- Rear wear accumulates in sector 2 traction zones; rear-axle temperature management important.
- May asphalt was re-laid before the 2023 race adding grip; soft compound is more viable than original layout.
- One-stop medium → hard is standard; undercut attempts at lap 15–22 common.

Key concerns:
- Turn 1 braking: Heavy stop from 280+ km/h; first-lap contact risk is high.
- Turn 11 exit: Key traction reference; rear instability here indicates degrading rear tires.
- Marbles: Off-line surface collects significant rubber marbles; overtaking off the racing line is punished.
