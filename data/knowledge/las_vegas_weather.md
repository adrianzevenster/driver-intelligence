# Las Vegas Weather Patterns
The Las Vegas Grand Prix is held in mid-November in the Nevada desert. Night-time desert temperatures in November are among the coldest of any F1 race, and this is the defining variable for both tire and battery strategy.

Conditions:
- Dry: Almost guaranteed. Las Vegas in November is desert-dry; rain probability is below 5%. No wet strategy planning required beyond having inters available per regulations.
- Temperature: Air 8–14°C at night. Track temperature 10–16°C at race start — the coldest race surface temperature on the F1 calendar. Only occasionally exceeded by a cold Melbourne morning session.
- Wind: Desert winds can be gusty (15–35 km/h) particularly in the first half of the night. The Strip and surrounding buildings create aerodynamic turbulence at ground level; wind direction through the main straight can vary.
- Track evolution: The street circuit gains rubber grip through the weekend; Friday is notoriously slippery.

Tire strategy in cold: Cold track surface extends tire life significantly compared to warm circuits. The thermal degradation mechanism is largely absent; mechanical wear is the limiting factor. However, compounds do not generate optimal grip below operating temperature; a poorly warmed tire in the first 3 laps will show elevated wear due to scrubbing. Monitor tire temperature actively in the first stint; expect the window to fully open on lap 3–4 rather than lap 1.
