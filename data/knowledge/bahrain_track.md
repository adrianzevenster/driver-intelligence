# Bahrain Track Intelligence
Sakhir is an abrasive desert surface with high rear-tire load through constant-radius medium-speed corners. Tire degradation is symmetrical but heavier on the rear axle than most circuits. Track evolution during the race is substantial because sand and dust blow onto the circuit before the race, and the rubber laid down rapidly transforms grip.

Key sectors:
- Sector 1: Turn 1 heavy braking, Turns 2–4 medium-speed sequence. Turn 4 is the primary rear-load corner; rear-right carries disproportionate stress through the apex.
- Sector 2: Turns 5–10 combination, including hairpin at Turn 10. Hairpin is the undercut/overcut pivot — cars can be undercut effectively up to lap 20 on Soft compound.
- Sector 3: Turns 11–15, fast final sector with DRS straight onto start-finish. Turn 11 braking is the second highest brake energy event.

Intervention notes: rear wear at Sakhir is the primary decision variable. If rear-left exceeds 55% before lap 18 on Medium compound, the window is open. Track evolution is fastest in laps 5–12 — do not penalise the driver for slow early laps on a new set; the circuit improves under them. If sand ingestion is reported at Turn 4 entry, grip loss is near-instantaneous; monitor for wheel-spin out of hairpin as early warning.
